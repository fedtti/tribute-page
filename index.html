Aaron Swartz
by
Sage Ross

Aaron Swartz (1986-2013) was a programmer, entrepreneur, writer, and activist. He’s mostly known as the father of the RSS format and the Markdown syntax, but he also was a strong Creative Commons supporter. Aaron co-founded Reddit with Steve Huffman, Alexis Ohanian, and Christopher Slowe in 2005: among other projects, he was involved in the foundation of Y Combinator too. As an activist, he created Demand Progress in 2010: he can be considered as a hacktivist pioneer yet back in 2008, when Aaron downloaded about 2.7 milion of documents from the PACER database; then the FBI prosecuted him and today it’s considered the main reason why he committed suicide in 2013 at the age of 26. He didn’t leave a note to explain his choice. Later that year, Aaron was found innocent and relieved of all the charges. However, we lost a passionate developer who could have been one of the most influent protagonists of the modern web. Thank you so much, Aaron! Learn more on the Aaron Swartz memorial website.
