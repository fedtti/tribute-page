Massimo Banzi
by
David Cuartielles

Massimo Banzi is an Italian interaction designer, educator and open source hardware advocate from Monza. Banzi is mostly known as the “father” of Arduino: a single-board microcontroller invented in 2003 at Interaction Design Institute di Ivrea (IDII) where he was employed as an associate professor. He’s currently teaching interaction design at Scuola Universitaria Professionale della Svizzera Italiana (SUPSI) in Lugano and is a visiting professor at Copenhagen Institute of Interaction Design (CIID). He also wrote Getting Started with Arduino published by O’Reilly® in March, 2009. Banzi is a regular contributor to the Italian edition of Wired and CheFuturo! – until its closing in 2016 – digital magazines.
